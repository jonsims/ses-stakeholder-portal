Environmental Justice Advocate — Data Centers

Director of Environmental Justice, Community Coalition (e.g., Piedmont Environmental Council, Virginia; or equivalent in Iowa, Ireland, Singapore)

Who You Represent

Residents and environmental organizations in communities hosting or targeted for large-scale data center construction. In Virginia's Loudoun and Prince William Counties — home to the world's densest data center cluster — this includes residents facing noise, visual impact, electricity rate increases, water competition, and exclusion from land use decisions. Nationally, more than 200 environmental organizations have aligned around calls for a moratorium.

What's at Stake
  • Electricity rate increases: grid transmission upgrades are socialized to all ratepayers
  • Water withdrawals from stressed regional aquifers and rivers
  • Loss of political power as data center revenues create economic dependency on the industry
  • False "green" claims by AI companies regarding renewable energy while driving fossil fuel generation at the grid margin
Your Priorities & Evidence
  • Meaningful community voice in data center siting and permitting decisions
  • Transparent disclosure of water usage, energy consumption, and grid impact
  • Equitable distribution of tax revenues generated by data centers to host communities
  • Preservation of agricultural land, historic sites, and viewsheds from industrial development
