Artisanal Cobalt Miner (DRC)

Artisanal and Small-Scale Miner (Creuseur), Lualaba Province, Democratic Republic of Congo - **Who they represent:** One of approximately 255,000 artisanal cobalt miners in the DRC — adults and children — who dig by hand in informal mines (known locally as "creuseurs"), often on the periphery of industrial concessions, selling raw ore to intermediaries for less than $2/day. - **Primary Interests:** Economic survival and income for their family in a context of extreme poverty and limited formal employment alternatives; physical safety in the absence of mine safety equipment or regulation; access to a fair share of the value generated by cobalt their labor extracts. - **Key Concerns (extraction/production stage):** Risk of tunnel collapse, respiratory disease from cobalt dust, and chemical exposure; child labor — including their own children — because household income depends on all family members working; debt bondage to ore-buying intermediaries; the 2024 court ruling absolving tech and automotive companies of liability for conditions in the supply chain; lack of a formal mechanism for their communities to benefit from the global EV transition. - **Likely Allies:** Stakeholder 6 (environmental justice advocate) - **Likely Opponents:** Stakeholders 1 (automaker executive, whose sourcing practices perpetuate the system) and 4 (battery materials company, whose profit margins depend on low-cost cobalt)
