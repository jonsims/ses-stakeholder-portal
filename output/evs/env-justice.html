Environmental Justice Advocate

Senior Researcher or Campaign Director, Environmental and Human Rights NGO (e.g., Earthworks, Global Witness, or Amnesty International's Business & Human Rights program)

Who You Represent

A coalition of affected communities, civil society organizations, and researchers who document and advocate against the environmental and human rights harms embedded in the EV battery supply chain — with particular focus on frontline communities in the DRC, Atacama, and Indonesian nickel-mining regions.

What's at Stake
  • The 2024 court ruling dismissing the DRC cobalt child labor lawsuit, signaling inadequate legal accountability for supply chain harms
  • Indonesia's coal-powered nickel smelting emitting SO2 and generating toxic tailings with minimal regulatory enforcement
  • Greenwashing by automakers who market EVs as "clean" while externalities are borne by communities in the Global South
  • Battery passport and traceability regimes that are voluntary or lack independent verification
  • Lithium mining water depletion threatening Atacama communities
  • Corporate capture of government regulators.
Your Priorities & Evidence
  • Binding corporate accountability for supply chain harms
  • Mandatory human rights and environmental due diligence legislation
  • FPIC requirements for mining on Indigenous lands
  • Child labor elimination in artisanal cobalt mining
  • Pollution remediation in nickel-processing communities
  • Ensuring the transition to EVs does not replicate the exploitation patterns of fossil fuel extraction in a new geography.
