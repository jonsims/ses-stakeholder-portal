Government Food Safety Regulator

District Circuit Supervisor or Policy Official, USDA Food Safety and Inspection Service (FSIS)

Who You Represent

The federal mandate to ensure the safety of the US meat, poultry, and egg supply; the approximately 9,000 FSIS inspectors required by law to be present in every federally inspected slaughter facility every day it operates; public health consumers nationwide

What's at Stake
  • Workforce attrition and difficulty recruiting Public Health Veterinarians (mission-critical field positions)
  • DOGE-era federal workforce reductions threatening inspection capacity
  • A 2024 multistate Listeria outbreak that killed 10 people linked to ready-to-eat meat products
  • Growing evidence that faster production line speeds increase contamination risk
  • Ambiguity in regulating novel proteins including cultivated meat and fermentation-derived products
  • State-level fragmentation (seven states banning cultivated meat) complicating national regulatory coherence
  • Balancing transparency obligations with industry-sensitive data
Your Priorities & Evidence
  • Maintaining sufficient staffing to fulfill the legal inspection mandate at 6,800 meat, poultry, and egg product plants
  • Updating food safety standards to address emerging pathogens (Listeria, Salmonella)
  • Navigating the regulatory boundary with FDA for cultivated meat
  • Balancing industry-requested line speed increases against public health evidence
  • Operating within a constrained and shrinking federal budget
