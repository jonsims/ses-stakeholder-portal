District Circuit Supervisor or Policy Official, USDA Food Safety and Inspection Service (FSIS) - **Who they represent:** The federal mandate to ensure the safety of the US meat, poultry, and egg supply; the approximately 9,000 FSIS inspectors required by law to be present in every federally inspected slaughter facility every day it operates; public health consumers nationwide - **Primary Interests:** Maintaining sufficient staffing to fulfill the legal inspection mandate at 6,800 meat, poultry, and egg product plants; updating food safety standards to address emerging pathogens (Listeria, Salmonella); navigating the regulatory boundary with FDA for cultivated meat; balancing industry-requested line speed increases against public health evidence; operating within a constrained and shrinking federal budget - **Key Concerns (extraction/production stage):** Workforce attrition and difficulty recruiting Public Health Veterinarians (mission-critical field positions); DOGE-era federal workforce reductions threatening inspection capacity; a 2024 multistate Listeria outbreak that killed 10 people linked to ready-to-eat meat products; growing evidence that faster production line speeds increase contamination risk; ambiguity in regulating novel proteins including cultivated meat and fermentation-derived products; state-level fragmentation (seven states banning cultivated meat) complicating national regulatory coherence; balancing transparency obligations with industry-sensitive data - **Likely Allies:** Stakeholder 1 (meatpacking executive — shares interest in a stable, predictable regulatory framework), Stakeholder 2 (contract farmer — regulatory structure provides some market order) - **Likely Opponents:** Stakeholder 3 (meatpacking worker — workers argue FSIS does not adequately enforce worker safety or line speed limits), Stakeholder 5 (animal welfare advocate — FSIS enforcement of humane handling at slaughter is viewed as insufficient)
