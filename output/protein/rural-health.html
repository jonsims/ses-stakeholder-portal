Rural Community Health Advocate

Community Health Researcher / Environmental Justice Advocate (e.g., North Carolina Environmental Justice Network, Iowa Citizens for Community Improvement)

Who You Represent

Rural residents — disproportionately Black, Indigenous, and low-income — living in close proximity to large CAFOs in states like North Carolina and Iowa; communities experiencing documented health disparities linked to CAFO siting; rural well users exposed to nitrate contamination

What's at Stake
  • North Carolina research documenting elevated mortality, kidney disease, and anemia near hog CAFOs
  • Higher asthma rates in children near Iowa hog barns
  • Nitrate contamination of rural wells from manure application
  • H₂S and ammonia emissions causing headaches, respiratory symptoms, and reduced quality of life
  • Disproportionate siting of CAFOs in communities of color
  • Limited CAFO regulation at the state level
  • Rollback of EPA rules governing CAFO reporting
Your Priorities & Evidence
  • Stricter siting regulations and setback distances for new CAFOs from homes, schools, and churches
  • Mandatory environmental impact assessments before new facility permits
  • Enforceable limits on lagoon application near waterways and private wells
  • Funding for community health monitoring
  • Legal accountability for corporations whose operations cause documented health damage
  • Recognition of CAFO siting as an environmental justice issue
