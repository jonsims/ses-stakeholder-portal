Mining Community Resident (Jiangxi, China)

Community Representative — Rare Earth Mining Village, Jiangxi Province, China - **Who they represent:** Residents of communities surrounding ion-adsorption rare earth mining operations in southern China — including farmers, fishing families, and village health workers — who experience the environmental externalities of mining without corresponding economic benefits; represents displaced and at-risk populations. - **Primary Interests:** Clean water and soil remediation funded by mining companies and the government; fair compensation for agricultural land rendered unproductive by mine drainage; meaningful participation in decisions about continued or expanded mining operations; transparent health monitoring and medical support for impacted community members. - **Key Concerns (extraction/production stage):** Ongoing leaching of heavy metals and nitrogen compounds into drinking water sources; government prioritization of rare earth revenues over community health; inadequate enforcement of environmental remediation commitments by mining companies; the 38-billion-yuan cleanup debt that remains largely unfunded; erosion and hillside instability threatening homes and farmland. - **Likely Allies:** Stakeholder 5 (Environmental NGO), Stakeholder 3 (Fab Worker) - **Likely Opponents:** Stakeholder 2 (Mining Company), Stakeholder 7 (Government Trade Policy Official), Stakeholder 1 (TV Brand Executive — indirectly sustains demand)
