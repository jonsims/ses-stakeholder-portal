Electronics Recycling / Right-to-Repair Advocate

Director of Policy — Electronics Recycling and Right-to-Repair Advocacy Organization (e.g., Electronics TakeBack Coalition, iFixit Policy Team)

Who You Represent

A coalition of interests including e-waste recycling processors, right-to-repair advocates, environmental justice organizations, and material recovery researchers; focused on the end-of-life stage but with a systemic view that connects material choices at the design/production stage to recyclability outcomes.

What's at Stake
  • Design decisions made during production that effectively make downstream recycling of critical materials (indium, rare earths) impossible at scale — particularly the fusion bonding of LCD glass stacks
  • The near-zero indium recycling rate despite indium's classification as critical and irreplaceable
  • Planned obsolescence via software cutoffs that drives premature TV replacement and increased mining demand
  • The gap between the 11 US states with EPR legislation and the rest of the country.
Your Priorities & Evidence
  • Mandatory Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) legislation that makes TV brands financially responsible for end-of-life collection and recycling
  • Design standards requiring TVs to be built for disassembly and material recovery
  • Minimum software support periods (currently being advanced in several US states and in the EU) to extend TV usable life and reduce replacement frequency
  • Development of economically viable indium and rare earth recovery from LCD panels
  • Opposition to design practices (adhesive bonding, proprietary fasteners) that prevent repair.
