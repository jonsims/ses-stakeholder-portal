Content Creator / Visual Artist or Writer

Independent Author, Illustrator, or Journalist Whose Work Was Scraped for AI Training

Who You Represent

The millions of artists, novelists, journalists, photographers, and other creative workers whose copyrighted work was included in AI training datasets without consent or compensation. This includes plaintiffs in active cases (New York Times, Authors Guild, Getty Images, musician coalitions) as well as the broader creative class affected by AI-generated content competing with their livelihoods.

What's at Stake
  • Unauthorized scraping of their specific works without attribution or payment
  • AI companies claiming fair use as blanket protection for commercial training on copyrighted material
  • Economic displacement: AI image generators trained on their style can produce unlimited competing images at near-zero marginal cost
  • NDA agreements in any settlements that prevent public disclosure of training data practices
Your Priorities & Evidence
  • Legal recognition that using copyrighted works for commercial AI training requires licensing or constitutes infringement
  • Compensation — either retroactive licensing or ongoing royalty structures — for use of their intellectual property
  • Transparency about what works were included in training datasets
  • Preservation of viable markets for human-created content as AI-generated alternatives proliferate
