Meatpacking Line Worker and/or Union Representative (e.g., United Food and Commercial Workers International Union — UFCW)

Who You Represent

The approximately 500,000 US meatpacking workers; disproportionately Latinx (34.9%), immigrant (42% foreign-born), and undocumented (estimated 30–50%); among the most economically vulnerable and physically endangered workers in the US economy

What's at Stake
  • Injury rates three times the national average
  • 27 workers per day hospitalized or amputated
  • 50% real wage decline since 1975
  • COVID-19 vulnerabilities from forced continued operations
  • Risk of deportation reducing willingness to report violations
  • Production line speed increases driven by productivity pressure
  • Limited OSHA enforcement in processing plants
Your Priorities & Evidence
  • Workplace safety (reduced line speeds, protective equipment, ergonomic reforms)
  • Legal immigration status protections that reduce exploitability
  • Living wages and the right to organize
  • Workers' compensation access
  • Protection from retaliation
  • An end to the structural use of immigration enforcement as a union-busting tool
