Government Critical Minerals Official

Deputy Assistant Secretary — Office of Critical Minerals and Supply Chain Resilience, US Department of Commerce (or equivalent EU/national government official)

Who You Represent

National government interests in securing supply chains for critical materials essential to both consumer electronics and national security/defense applications; responsible for translating geopolitical risk into policy instruments.

What's at Stake
  • China's demonstrated willingness to use rare earth export controls as geopolitical leverage (2025 episode)
  • The inadequacy of current recycling infrastructure as a domestic supply offset
  • The long timelines required to permit and build alternative mines and processing facilities
  • The risk that purely market-based approaches will not deliver supply chain security at the required pace.
Your Priorities & Evidence
  • Reducing dependence on Chinese rare earth supply and processing
  • Building domestic or allied-nation alternatives (e.g., Lynas in Australia, Mountain Pass in California, Rare Earth processing partnerships with Canada)
  • Using trade policy (tariffs, export controls, CHIPS Act-adjacent programs) to reshore critical materials production
  • Ensuring rare earth supply security is addressed in bilateral trade negotiations.
