Autoworker Union Representative

Regional Director, United Auto Workers (UAW), or Shop Steward, IG Metall (Germany)

Who You Represent

The interests of workers employed in vehicle assembly and battery manufacturing — both established ICE-era workers facing an uncertain transition, and newer workers at EV and battery plants where union representation is being actively contested.

What's at Stake
  • Tesla's sustained resistance to union organizing (NLRB complaints, alleged retaliatory firings)
  • The use of temporary or foreign contract workers at battery plants in lieu of permanent unionized positions
  • The high capital intensity of battery manufacturing potentially replacing labor with automation
  • GM and Ford EV division losses leading to production pauses and layoffs
  • Whether IRA domestic manufacturing incentives translate into good union jobs or simply subsidize non-union facilities.
Your Priorities & Evidence
  • Job security during the EV transition
  • Fair wages and benefits at new battery gigafactories
  • Union recognition and collective bargaining rights at Tesla and other non-union EV manufacturers
  • Ensuring that the green transition does not come at the cost of worker rights and pay
  • Safety standards in battery manufacturing environments (chemical exposures, repetitive strain injuries).
